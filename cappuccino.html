Cappuccino Double Paceline Quality

A clean double paceline is a smooth step-wave: a ~21 mph slow line and a ~22 mph fast line, rotating steadily. This ranks every logged Cappuccino by how well that line actually formed — measured from the long flat run up MacArthur to the base of Anglers.

IND — your own steadiness. A formed line lets you hold a steady pull; a broken one means constant surge-and-coast on the wheel.
ROT — the group's rotation. Rewards a tight speed band near the 21–22 target; penalises both the accordion and running too hot.
Q — the headline, (IND + ROT) / 2. 100 = a beautiful steady line. Low = it never came together.

How it's scored

IND comes from your power trace (jerk = how often watts jump >50W second-to-second, plus variability and coasting) — or, on rides with no power meter, from cadence (which tracks the power chop closely). ROT comes from the speed trace: how tightly it held a band, and whether that band sat near the 21–22 mph social target rather than running hot. Both are scored relative to the field of logged Cappuccino rides. Segment: MacArthur Blvd — Walhonding to base of Anglers (~5.7 mi).

The two numbers are deliberately separate — a ride can be a steady pull through a chaotic pack (high IND, low ROT) or a calm pack you personally surged all over, e.g. on gravel (low IND, high ROT).
